#### **HabitKit Is Going Crazy**

As you probably know, my most important app is [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app) - a habit tracking app for iOS and Android. And let me tell you: End of December and the start of January are absolutely crazy times for habit tracking apps. Like, really crazy. Everybody is making New Year’s resolutions and trying to improve their lives. They want to exercise more, eat healthier, read more books, drink less alcohol. And to achieve all of this, they are looking for tools and apps to help them stay on track.

Over the past three years, [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app) has reached a really solid position on the App Store and Google Play in terms of ranking for relevant keywords (”habit tracker” for example). This leads to exploding download and revenue numbers every year around this time. It’s like Christmas all over again, just for indie hackers. I couldn’t be happier with the results!

Let me give you some actual numbers because I know you like those: [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app) made a crazy jump in terms of MRR alone. We went from $28k to $31.5k in just 10 days. Ten days! That’s $3,500 in new monthly recurring revenue added in less than two weeks. That’s huge progress for me and I really hope that many of the people who bought the app will stay on track to build great habits and change their lives for the better. That’s the whole point of the app after all.

#### **New Goals (But Not Too Grand)**

This week was all about easing back into my work habits after the holiday break. You know how it is: you take some time off and suddenly it feels impossible to sit down and actually work again. I started doing regular Pomodoro sessions with [FocusKit](https://tryfocuskit.com) already and it helped me a lot with maintaining focus again. Always a great feeling when my own app is helping me improve my own life.

I also sat down and really thought about my goals for the year. And I gained a valuable insight: Don’t think about GRAND GOALS. 

Let me explain what I mean. At the end of last year, I was already thinking about revamping my existing apps completely. New architecture! New design! Everything better! Or maybe adding new apps to my portfolio? A whole new app that does something completely different! Sounds exciting, right?

But here’s the thing: I actually want to dedicate the next few months to stabilizing my already existing apps. I have some cool updates and features planned for [FocusKit](https://tryfocuskit.com) and I will make sure to follow through on my plans. Small and consistent updates are so much better than huge rebuilds that never happen because you lose motivation in the middle of the project. We’ve all been there. You start with so much energy and then... nothing. The project sits there, half-finished, making you feel guilty every time you think about it.

You always think that once you reached the grand goal (”When I finally rewrote the architecture of [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app)...”) everything will be fun and good. Like there’s this magical finish line and once you cross it, work becomes easy and enjoyable forever. But the hard truth is: It’s always hard to continue work on existing projects. The grass always looks greener on the other side. A new project sounds more exciting than fixing bugs in your current one. But the boring work is often the most important work.

So my plan for the next few months is simple: Keep shipping small updates. Keep improving what already works. And resist the temptation to start something completely new just because it sounds more fun. Let’s see if I can stick to this plan. I’ll keep you updated!
