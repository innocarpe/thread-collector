This week I kept working hard on my new app, and now there are only two weeks left until my self-imposed deadline for launching the Pomodoro timer. Time feels super short and there’s still a lot waiting on my to-do list, which is REALLY stressful. But in the end, I’m glad that I gave myself this deadline. It helps me to stay motivated and pushes me to focus only on the most important things that really matter for the first release. It’s easy to fall into the trap of being comfortable when you already have a well-selling app on the App Store, so doing challenging things like this is a great way to stay sharp.

Here is everything I did this week:

At the beginning of the week, I worked on a bunch of smaller tasks just to get into the right mood for building. I added some minor features here and there, for example, I made sure that the app asks users for a review on the App Store after they completed their first focus session. This part actually was so much easier compared to doing this for my previous apps (made with Flutter), so that was nice! I also improved the way the app handles permissions, trying to make it as easy as possible for the user. I also implemented adjusting the timer durations, so users will be able to change the default lengths of their focus, short-break, and long-break sessions.

I spent some time thinking about how to handle the topics “analytics” and “crash reporting” in the app. In the end, I decided not to use Firebase Crashlytics or any other kind of analytics tool right now. I want to keep things simple and just use the App Store’s own diagnostics tools for launch. The good thing about this choice is that I don’t need to ask for extra permissions, and I can keep the privacy section of the App Store listing really minimal, which I think is important these days.

I also spent a lot of time experimenting with the design and different color combinations for the app. For me, this was the hardest part because I still haven’t found something that really feels right. I keep thinking about what the main screen should look like since this is what users will see the most. The colors that I have right now for the session types - blue for focus, green for short break, and orange for long break - don’t really fit well with the rest of the design in my opinion. Every time I open the app, I feel like something is just a bit off, which is frustrating.

At one point, I even tried adding a crazy animated background. It actually looked sort of cool and made the app feel more lively. But after using it for a while, I realized it was too much and could distract people when they try to concentrate, so I decided to remove it again. Maybe I will bring this back in a future update when I find a way to make it less distracting. For now, I’m still searching for a better style and colors that really work with the whole app.

I also did some keyword research for the App Store this week, which was really important. I previously haven’t looked at the market even once. [I used Astro, which is honestly my favorite tool for this](https://tryastro.app/?aff=15APy). It’s especially good if you want to launch an app but don’t want to pay hundreds of dollars for something like Appfigures. TryAstro allows you to track as many keywords as you like, and it is definitely the best tool when you don’t have a big budget.

The Pomodoro timer area on the App Store is super competitive, but I already have some experience bringing a new app to the Top-3 of the App Store in a highly competitive market (Habit Tracking for [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app)) and I hope I can do it again. I still believe there’s a chance for new apps if you can stand out somehow. Right now, my dream would be to land in the top 5 results for “Pomodoro Timer” one day. I know it’s not easy, but with good keywords and a bit of luck, I am sure I can make it happen.

I also finished writing a nice App Store description for the app and filled out all the privacy forms that Apple needs. There are still some things left to do in App Store Connect, like making the App Store screenshots and picking the final price for the in-app purchase and the subscriptions. These feel like the last big steps before I can finally submit everything and launch. It’s crazy to think how close it is now, but I also feel a bit nervous that there are still small things left.

One thing I am really happy about is that I managed to find a super talented designer for the app icon. His name is [Matthew Skiles](https://matthewskiles.com/) (he is a legend in the iOS developer community) and I am really excited to work with him. I think having a good-looking icon is super important for standing out in the App Store, so I can’t wait to see what ideas he will come up with. Hopefully, the new icon will help make the app look and feel much more professional.
