Welcome back to another issue of my weekly indie log! This was a crazy productive week. I finished a major [FocusKit](https://tryfocuskit.com) update (releasing next week!), got [HabitKit](https://habitkit.app) back into a development-ready state, and made more progress on my secret macOS project. Multiple projects moving forward at the same time, which is exciting and exhausting simultaneously.

#### **🛠️ Development Corner**

A lot happened this week, so let me break it down project by project.

**FocusKit 1.3 is done.** The update adds Apple Watch integration, which I’ve been working on for the past few weeks. I’m really happy with how it turned out, and the feedback from beta testers has been great so far. I think this will be a solid addition to the product and improve the workflow for a lot of users who want to track their focus time without pulling out their phone. Getting this shipped feels really good.
[이미지: Image]

**HabitKit is back in business.** I finally upgraded the project to the latest Flutter version and updated all dependencies. This doesn’t sound exciting, but it was long overdue. Work has been piling up for HabitKit because I was so focused on FocusKit and the secret macOS project over the past weeks. Now I feel like I have a solid foundation again to iterate on the product. It’s really hard to find a good balance of time allocation when you’re running multiple projects at the same time. HabitKit is still my most important project, and it deserves more attention.

**Secret macOS project is getting closer.** I finished another test version this week and I’m really proud of the result so far. It’s a native macOS app that utilizes Apple’s liquid glass APIs, which I personally love. The goal is to release it sometime in March or April. I’m working on this project with two friends, so I don’t have complete control over the timeline, but things are moving in the right direction.

#### **📊 Business Updates**

FocusKit MRR is still stalling at around $120. The App Store ranking isn’t great yet, but the app is still relatively young, so I doubt it will rank for the bigger keywords this soon. The most important things right now are: listening to user feedback, gathering reviews on the App Store, and improving the product. That’s all I can really control.

On the bright side, one-time-purchase revenue is looking decent. The app made about $600 in the past 28 days, which is pretty cool for a project this young. I’m also planning to run some App Store screenshot A/B tests soon. I have a feeling that a clean white background with black text could have a better conversion rate than my current design. We’ll see how that goes.

Here’s the big win of the week though: revenue for February 2026 is higher than February 2025. This is huge for me because the past two months were slightly down compared to their respective months from last year. That dip was probably caused by the MKBHD video mention of HabitKit (from “The Studio”) which boosted revenue and downloads artificially back then. Now it feels like we’re actually growing organically again, which is awesome and really reassuring.

I’m also thinking about increasing prices for FocusKit. Haven’t made a final decision yet, but it’s something I want to experiment with.

#### **💡 Indie Insights**

**AI tooling keeps getting better.** I think Cursor is the real GOAT right now. It lets you pick whatever AI model you want, gives you full control over the code, and the workflow just feels right. Highlight a code snippet, throw it into the agent context, done. The progress I’m making across all my projects right now would not be possible without it. Software engineering expertise is becoming less and less of a bottleneck. What matters now is focus, clear thinking, and knowing what to build.

**OpenClaw reality check.** Remember when I was super excited about my OpenClaw setup? The reality is that the maintenance cost is just too high. It breaks often, and when I task it to debug itself, it burns through tokens like crazy. Perplexity released an alternative called Perplexity Computer which looks interesting, but I’m hesitant to sign up for another $200/month subscription just to try some AI tools. I’m sure more polished agent solutions will come over time, making the whole concept less maintenance-intensive. For now, the hype has cooled down for me a little bit.

**Focus is the real superpower.** I keep coming back to this, but it’s really true. In a world where you can build almost anything with the right tools and a bit of persistence, the bottleneck is not skill anymore. It’s focus. Knowing what you want and pursuing your projects with real motivation is what separates people who ship from people who don’t. This week I had some days with zero appointments, and the difference in output compared to days with scattered meetings was massive. I also realized that I slipped back into some bad habits around screen time and distractions. It’s always like a landslide. You open one gate, and the flood comes. You need to stay disciplined on all fronts, all the time. Deep life habits like going offline more, reading more, and limiting social media to a fixed 20-minute window per day are things I want to get back to.

**Journaling is underrated.** I’ve been writing in my journal daily for a while now, and it really is a superpower. It helps me reflect on my progress and where I want to go. The past couple of weeks have been super work-intensive and I made a ton of progress, but writing things down every day keeps me grounded and intentional. If you’re not journaling yet, I really recommend trying it.

#### **🎯 Goals for Next Week**

Next week I want to start planning the next big FocusKit update. I think it will be all about Home Screen Widgets. I also want to continue with HabitKit development and finally tackle some long-due performance optimizations. Lots of work ahead, but I’m feeling motivated and ready to keep shipping.
