Me realising that i make a mistake :)

Becoming an indie developer is exciting. You finally have the freedom to build what you want, work on your own schedule, and (hopefully) make money doing what you love.

But here’s the truth: **I wasted a lot of time making dumb mistakes that slowed me down, drained my motivation, and cost me real money.**

If you’re just starting or you’ve been stuck in the cycle of unfinished projects this is for you.

Here are **the 5 my biggest mistakes I made as an indie developer and how to avoid them.**

#### **Mistake #1: Waiting Too Long to Start**

For years, I told myself:

_“I need more experience. I need a better idea. I need to be fully prepared.”_

Reality check? **You’re never 100% ready.**

The longer you wait, the harder it gets. There’s no _perfect_ moment only _missed_ opportunities.

Even if your first project flops, it’s still progress. You learn, you improve, and you move forward.

✅ Start **now.** Build something small. Launch an MVP. **Get real-world feedback.**

Your first project won’t be your best. But it’ll be the most important because it gets you moving.

#### **Mistake #2: Skipping Validation**

I never struggled with ideas. My problem? **I built apps no one actually wanted.**

I’d spend **months coding**, only to launch an app that got **zero** traction. Why? Because I never **validated the idea.**

Here’s the hard truth:

- Just because YOU think an app is useful **doesn’t mean others do.**
- If no one is excited before you build it, **no one will be after.**

✅ Before writing a single line of code, **validate your idea.**

• Create a simple **landing page** and track interest.

• Post on **Reddit, Twitter, IndieHackers.** Ask people if they’d use it.

• Offer a **pre-launch signup** to gauge demand.

If no one bites? **Move on.** Validation first, development second.

#### **Mistake #3: Trying to Make It Perfect**

I wasted **way too much time** making things _pixel-perfect._

Perfect UI, perfect animations, perfect onboarding… Meanwhile, my app was **never actually done.**

The hard truth? **Users don’t care about perfection.**

- They care if your app **solves a problem.**
- They won’t notice the micro-adjustments you obsess over.

And the worst part? **Every delay costs you money and motivation.**

✅ **Ship early, fix later.** A working app with bugs is better than a perfect app that never launches.

✅ **80/20 Rule:** 80% of your users will use only **20% of your features.** Prioritise what matters.

#### **Mistake #4: Jumping Between Projects**

I had **shiny object syndrome.**

A new idea popped up? I dropped everything and started working on it. Again. And again.

**End result?** A graveyard of unfinished projects.

The dopamine rush of starting something new is addictive. But success **doesn’t come from starting it comes from finishing.**

✅ Pick **one** project and **stick with it.**

✅ Set a rule: **No new projects until the current one is finished.**

✅ Keep a **‘next ideas’ list** so you don’t lose good ideas but don’t act on them _yet._

The most successful indie devs? **They finish.**

#### **Mistake #5: Neglecting Marketing**

I thought, _“If I build something great, people will find it.”_

Biggest mistake ever.

- **A product without marketing is invisible.**
- Just launching your app and moving to the next one? **A waste of effort.**
- If you’re not marketing while building, **you’re already behind.**

I used to love **building.** But the hard truth is, **marketing is half the job — maybe more.**

✅ **Start marketing before you launch.**

- Share your journey on **Twitter, TikTok, Reddit.**
- Build a **waitlist.**
- Post updates. Get people involved early.

✅ **Treat marketing like a core feature.**

If no one knows your app exists, it doesn’t matter how good it is.

#### **Final Thoughts: Learn From My Mistakes**

If you’re an indie dev, don’t waste years like I did.

- Start **now.**
- Validate **before** coding.
- Ship **before it’s perfect.**
- Finish **before jumping to the next idea.**
- Market **as hard as you build.**

If this hit home, **share your biggest indie dev mistake in the comments.** Let’s help each other build smarter, faster, and more successfully.
